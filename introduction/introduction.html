jour·nal·ism

/ˈjərnlˌizəm/
noun
the activity or profession of writing for newspapers, magazines, or news websites or preparing news to be broadcast.

responsibilities of a journalist include pitching stories, interviewing sources, researching, writing in accordance with AP style, taking photos, speaking truth and minimizing harm. journalists work for newspapers, television and radio stations, and magazines.
